As defined below, the KIR recogonises three categories of data.

If you need to work with category A or B data, please contact the KIR Research Computing Administrator. If the IG situation is unclear a Data Subject Anonymity Assessment will be performed. Special arrangements are made for controlling access to category A or B datasets to ensure that the KIR meets its information governance obligations. For Category B data, access is typically restricted by secondary group membership. For Category A data, a seperate workspace may be created. The KIR maintains an asset register for Category A and B datasets.

>You must ensure that the KIR Research Computing Administrator is aware of all Category A and B datasets that you are working with, or that you plan to work with.
{.is-warning}

>All users working with human data must be familiar with the concept of personal information under the GDPR (https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/personal-information-what-is-it/what-is-personal-information-a-guide/ ) and be aware that “It is an offence for a person knowingly or recklessly to re-identify information that is de-identified personal data without the consent of the controller responsible for de-identifying the personal data” (see DPA2018 legislation:  
https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2018/12/section/171?view=plain ).
{.is-warning}

> Pseudoanonymisation is "[the] processing of personal data in such a manner that the personal data can no longer be attributed to a specific data subject without the use of additional information, provided that such additional information is kept separately and is subject to technical and organisational measures to ensure that the personal data are not attributed to an identified or identifiable natural person." (see: https://ico.org.uk/media2/migrated/4019579/chapter-3-anonymisation-guidance.pdf)
{.is-info}

## Data categories

The descriptions given below are indicative rather than definitive: if you are not sure which category of data your project(s) will fall into, please discuss this with the KIR Research Computing Administrator. In such cases it may be necessary to complete a Data Subject Anonymity Assessment (DSAA).


### Category A – Identifiable human datasets

Identifiable human data or datasets containing non-anonymous personal information under UK GDPR/DPA2018 (e.g. genetic, health, biometric). This includes pseudo anonymised datasets where the identification of subjects by a motivated individual is reasonably likely. Examples of this type of data include:

*	Whole genome sequencing data with non-anonymised identifiers
*	Health data (e.g. age, weight) with non-anonymised identifiers
*	Pseudo-anonymised data where there is significant risk of de-identification by a motivated individual, e.g. (i) if the identity of members of the cohorts is public knowledge [e.g. if samples were collected from a football team], or (ii) the dataset is very large in scale.


### Category B – Restricted use datasets or pseudo-anonymised human datasets 

This includes: (i) data from human subjects that is pseudo-anonymised such that the identification of subjects by a motivated individual is not reasonably likely (e.g. whole-genome DNA-sequencing/genotyping data) and/or (ii) data obtained from third parties to which access is restricted by access agreements or CDA/NDA. Examples of this type of data include:

*	Genotyping data with pseudo-anonymised identifiers from an unknown set of subjects
*	Health data with pseudo-anonymised identifiers from an unknown set of subjects
*	Anonymous data obtained under NDA 


### Category C – Non identifiable data.

Including (i) data from model systems, (ii) non-patient generated common cell lines, (iii) non-identifiable human data. Examples of this type of data include:

•	Genomics experiments performed on mouse models.
•	Pseudoanonymised human data comprised of summary statistics from an unknown set of subjects
•	Pseudoanonymised human bulk/single-cell RNA-sequencing data from an unknown set of subjects
